At the Olympics 2016 in Rio, [11,238] athletes participated.

This huge graph shows all the athletes participating in the Olympics 2016. However, it becomes clear that many of the athletes do not have any links to any other athlete. This is due to many athletes only having very little information on the Wikipedia page, and no link to any other athlete competing at the Olympics. Even though these athletes might be really interesting, we will move them from the graph, since Network science only makes sense if there is a network (e.g. nodes and links connected).

Connected Athlete Network

Now only the Gian Component of the Athletes network is shown and the community algorithm is ran, to visualize the communities in the Athletes Network:

Now all the nodes are connected to each other, and quickly we can identify some of the commnities from the big Olympic Network. Several other communities are easy to observe. This is called a social network, where there are already easy to spot communities. It is obvious that the group of athletes that perform the same sport are in a community together, or the athletes that have often been playing against each other, are in communities together.

Cliqus within the communities

Looking at the network, some communities seem very dense, where some are more spread out.

How can that be?

In Network science there are something called strong and weak communities. This is defined by how many cliques the community has. A cliques in a network corresponds to a complete (fully connected) subgraph, where all the nodes are connected to each other. If a community is having many cliques, and thereby makes every node within the community have more links to each other than to other nodes outside the community, it is defined as a strong community. It the opposite occur, the community is a weak community. Looking at the connected athletes network, community XX and XX are cleary strong communities, only determined by the density of the comunity. But investigating the number of cliques inside the two sommunities also revieal that this is true. The biggest clique in all of the olympic network is found in community XXX.

This clique are all famous female tennis players. All of these tennis players are linking to all the other tennis players in the cliniqe, which is what is called fully connected. Try visit [Serena Williams] Wikipedia page, and see if you can find the 16 female tennis players that her page links to.

But can we detect the communities even more, and maybe spot which sport isolates athletes from other sports, and which sports that might connect atheltes accross sports.
